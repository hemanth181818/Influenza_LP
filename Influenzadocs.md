# Influenza: Creator Marketing Operating System

## 1. What Influenza Is
Influenza is a comprehensive creator marketing operating system designed to replace scattered spreadsheets, chat threads, and manual tracking[cite: 2]. It provides a single, unified platform to manage the entire creator lifecycle—from initial discovery to final revenue attribution[cite: 2]. 

With the seamless integration of the Creator Marketplace, Influenza now serves as an end-to-end engine for scouting influencers, prioritizing product pushes based on live inventory, managing budgets, and measuring direct commerce outcomes.

## 2. Core Business Outcomes
*   **Targeted Scouting:** Discover and vet influencers directly through Meta's Creator Marketplace without leaving the ecosystem.
*   **Outcome-Based Measurement:** Connect creator awareness metrics directly to commerce outcomes and affiliate-attributed orders[cite: 2].
*   **Inventory-Driven Marketing:** Make product-push decisions based on live stock pressure, inventory value, and days of holding (DOH) rather than instinct[cite: 2].
*   **Operational Velocity:** Accelerate campaign planning, streamline the approval flow, and automate shipment tracking for creator product seeding[cite: 1, 2].
*   **Financial Governance:** Maintain strict visibility on committed spend, remaining monthly budgets, and cost-per-view (CPV) efficiency[cite: 2].
*   **Centralized Reporting:** Faster reporting for leadership, marketing, finance, and operations[cite: 2].

## 3. Key Questions Answered
*   Which influencers match our audience demographics and engagement requirements via Meta's database?
*   Which products require immediate marketing pushes to align with broader inventory planning and live stock pressure?[cite: 2]
*   What is the real-time status of our monthly creator budget?[cite: 2]
*   How fast are campaign views accumulating, and what is the content's shelf-life?[cite: 2]
*   Which creators drive pure awareness versus actual affiliate conversions?[cite: 2]
*   What is the Turn Around Time (TAT) for creator product shipments?[cite: 1]

## 4. User Roles and Access
*   **Admin/Owner:** Used by leadership or operations control to view full dashboards, manage monthly budgets, review high-level approvals, and dictate system governance[cite: 1, 2].
*   **Management/Growth:** Executes day-to-day campaign planning, scouts creators via Meta, submits new collaboration requests, and monitors affiliate performance[cite: 1, 2].
*   **Operations (Warehouse):** Manages the logistics of product seeding, tracking shipments, and monitoring dispatch TAT[cite: 1].
*   **Developer:** Maintains system integrations, including GoAffPro syncing and Meta API connectivity[cite: 1].

## 5. Core System Modules

### 5.1 Meta Creator Marketplace (Influencer Scouting)
*   **Purpose:** To discover, filter, and evaluate potential creator partners directly within Influenza.
*   **Functionality:** Interfaces directly with Meta to pull live creator profiles, follower demographics, platform presence (Instagram/YouTube), and engagement metrics. 
*   **Value:** Eliminates the need for external scouting tools and instantly funnels prospective creators into the Influenza approval and campaign pipeline.

### 5.2 Dashboard & Analytics
*   **Purpose:** Provides a live snapshot of the entire creator program[cite: 2].
*   **Functionality:** Displays total views, platform splits (Instagram vs. YouTube), active creator counts, total spend, and CPV[cite: 2]. It visualizes monthly view trends and category splits[cite: 1].
*   **Value:** Delivers an immediate answer to "What is happening right now?" for leadership and marketing teams[cite: 2].

### 5.3 Product Recommendations & Budget Control
*   **Purpose:** Aligns creator marketing spend with actual business and inventory needs[cite: 2].
*   **Functionality:** Ranks products based on stock pressure, daily run rate (DRR), and inventory value to highlight what needs a marketing push[cite: 2]. Tracks the monthly budget (e.g., ₹10.00 L), showing committed versus available funds alongside specific deficit values tied up in slow stock[cite: 1, 2].
*   **Value:** Prevents budget overruns and ensures marketing efforts support slow-moving or high-value inventory[cite: 2].

### 5.4 Campaigns
*   **Purpose:** The operational pipeline for all ongoing creator work[cite: 2].
*   **Functionality:** Tracks creator, asset type, schedule dates, posting status, and whether the deal is paid or barter[cite: 1, 2]. 
*   **Value:** Replaces manual follow-ups with a traceable, controlled campaign board[cite: 2].

### 5.5 Creator Intake Hub
*   **Purpose:** Manages inbound interest from creators wanting to collaborate[cite: 1].
*   **Functionality:** Connects to a shareable public application page[cite: 1]. Captures creator details, platform links, audience demographics, product interests, and content types[cite: 1].
*   **Value:** Centralizes inbound leads for quick review, approval, or rejection by the management team[cite: 1].

### 5.6 Approvals
*   **Purpose:** Gives admin a review layer before the team moves ahead[cite: 2].
*   **Functionality:** Supports approving or rejecting direct requests, reviewing submitted creator profiles, and capturing rejection notes[cite: 1, 2].
*   **Value:** Ensures the team can move fast while the process stays controlled and traceable[cite: 2].

### 5.7 Shipment Tracking
*   **Purpose:** Connects creator agreements to physical product delivery[cite: 1].
*   **Functionality:** Monitors carrier details (e.g., BlueDart, Delhivery), tracking numbers, submission times, and dispatch times[cite: 1]. Calculates average Turn Around Time (TAT) to ensure creators receive gear promptly[cite: 1].
*   **Value:** Gives operations and marketing teams visibility into fulfillment bottlenecks[cite: 1].

### 5.8 Affiliates & Commerce
*   **Purpose:** Connects top-of-funnel awareness to bottom-of-funnel revenue[cite: 2].
*   **Functionality:** Syncs directly with external affiliate platforms (e.g., GoAffPro) to track active affiliates, total orders, generated revenue, average order value, and paid commissions[cite: 1, 2]. Highlights top-performing products sold via affiliates[cite: 1].
*   **Value:** Proves the ROI of the creator program by highlighting exactly who drives commerce[cite: 2].

### 5.9 Creator Profile & Directory
*   **Purpose:** Provides a searchable list of creators and a full performance view for individual creators[cite: 2].
*   **Functionality:** Shows follower size, engagement indicators, spend history, campaign history, and posted content links[cite: 1, 2].
*   **Value:** Helps teams decide whether to reuse, upgrade, pause, or reactivate a creator relationship[cite: 2].

### 5.10 Export & User Management
*   **Purpose:** Makes reporting easy to share and controls system access[cite: 2].
*   **Functionality:** Allows exports of deliverables and influencers via CSV, and lets admins create, activate, or deactivate user roles[cite: 1, 2].

## 6. The Operating Workflow

1.  **Analyze & Prioritize:** Open the Dashboard to review current performance[cite: 2]. Check Product Recommendations to identify which inventory requires a marketing push based on DOH, DRR, and overall inventory planning[cite: 2]. Set the monthly budget[cite: 2].
2.  **Scout & Discover:** Utilize the Meta Creator Marketplace integration to find influencers whose audiences align with the prioritized products.
3.  **Review Inbound Leads:** Check the Creator Intake Hub module for any organic applications that fit the current month's product focus[cite: 1].
4.  **Submit & Approve:** Draft campaign proposals for scouted or inbound creators and submit them through the Approvals queue for leadership sign-off[cite: 2].
5.  **Seed Product:** Once approved, trigger the logistics workflow. Use the Shipment Tracking module to ensure the creator receives their barter or review unit within the target TAT[cite: 1].
6.  **Manage the Campaign:** Track the deliverable in the Campaigns board, updating statuses from scheduled to posted[cite: 2].
7.  **Measure Outcome:** Monitor live views, CPV, and engagement in the Creator Profile[cite: 2]. Cross-reference with the Affiliates module to track actual orders and revenue generated by that specific campaign[cite: 1, 2]. Export data as needed for leadership and finance[cite: 2].

## 7. Glossary

*   **Deliverable:** One piece of creator content, such as one Instagram reel or one YouTube video[cite: 2].
*   **Barter / SP:** A product-only collaboration[cite: 2].
*   **Paid:** A cash-paid collaboration[cite: 2].
*   **CPV (Cost Per View):** How much paid spend was required to generate one view[cite: 2].
*   **DRR (Daily Run Rate):** How fast a product is moving[cite: 2].
*   **DOH (Days of Holding):** How long current stock may last at the current movement rate[cite: 2].
*   **TAT (Turn Around Time):** Time from order submission to dispatch for creator product shipments[cite: 1].
*   **Content Shelf-Life:** How long a piece of creator content continues to deliver views after it goes live[cite: 2].
*   **Affiliate-Attributed Conversion:** An order or revenue event linked back to an affiliate code, link, or creator-led affiliate path[cite: 2].
